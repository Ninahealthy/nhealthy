In order for a patient to receive compensation from a negligent medical professional, the patient must prove three crucial elements beyond reasonable doubt. These are that the professional is responsible for the patient's injuries; the professional failed in his or her duty; and that the patient's injury was caused as a result of the negligence of the medical professional. Additionally, the patient must establish that the injury was caused as a direct result of the negligent act or omissions of the medical professional.It's not necessary to hire a Pennsylvania medical malpractice lawyer to represent you if you want to recover compensation from a medical negligence claim. This is because they understand that most insurance companies have lawyers working on contingency fees, so if they don't win the compensation that they're entitled to, they do not have to pay their client. Additionally, these highly experienced and skilled attorneys work diligently on contingency fees only, so they only get paid if they win their case - and in many cases, they do.
